## 10 observations from this session

1. The first hypothesis ("falta de conhecimento e ferramentas") was solution-framed, not problem-framed. Rejected in under 10 minutes with basic market analysis.

2. The real problem is a three-layer barrier: emotional (fear, shame, low confidence), cognitive (complexity, operational knowledge), and trust (distrust in banks, advisors, gurus). These are not the same and require different interventions.

3. Perceived complexity is the most frequent barrier (35% of Reddit mentions). Fear of loss is the most intense (5/5). The most common is not the same as the most important.

4. The ANBIMA data is the strongest signal in the research: 33% saved in 2025, only 10% invested in financial products. This validates the saving-to-investing gap at national scale.

5. The workaround is "good enough" — Nubank caixinha, poupança, dinheiro parado. People are not desperate. The pain is real but not acute enough to force action without a trigger.

6. Willingness to pay is completely unvalidated. The entire segment defaults to free solutions (Reddit, YouTube, forums). This is the most critical open question before any build decision.

7. The segment skews 19–25 in external data, younger than the original 18–50 hypothesis. The 25–50 group needs separate investigation.

8. Solution pull is a consistent personal pattern. The impulse to build appeared as soon as the pain was validated, before WTP was answered. Named and managed in session.

9. A bounded experiment ($100 + 1-week MVP) is sound in principle, but only if it includes a real payment mechanic. Measuring signups is not the same as measuring willingness to pay.

10. Discovery methodology requires staying in problem space until validation is complete. Solution questions that appear during problem discovery should be logged as open questions, not answered immediately.

## How this changes how I think about product

Problem framing is not the same as solution framing. "Falta de ferramentas acessíveis" is already pointing at what to build. "Barreira emocional, cognitiva e de confiança" is pointing at what to understand. The second framing produces a better product.

The gap between saving and investing is not a niche problem. ANBIMA data makes it a national-scale gap. The product doesn't need to manufacture demand — it needs to convert existing intent.
